UTMOS i UTMOS is an automatic MOS predictor trained on human speech quality ratings. It estimates naturalness on a 1–5 scale without human listeners. A score ≥ 3.5 indicates the adversarial audio still sounds natural.
Predicted MOS · threshold ≥3.5
PESQ i Perceptual Evaluation of Speech Quality. Measures perceptual distortion between the adversarial and ground truth audio. Here it is normalised to [0, 1] and inverted: 0 = identical to GT, 1 = maximally distorted. Success threshold ≤ 0.20 means the audio remains perceptually close to the original.
Perceptual quality · threshold ≤0.20
SET_OVERLAP i Fraction of checkwords shared between the ground truth and the ASR transcription of the adversarial audio, after lemmatisation. Checkwords are words remaining after removing function words (stopwords). 0 = no shared checkwords, 1 = identical. Success threshold ≤ 0.50.
Checkword overlap · threshold ≤0.50
SBERT Semantic Similarity i Cosine similarity between Sentence-BERT embeddings of the ground truth text and the ASR transcription of the adversarial audio. Measures how much the meaning diverged. 1 = identical meaning, 0 = completely unrelated. Success threshold ≤ 0.60 indicates meaningful semantic divergence.
ASR vs ground truth · threshold ≤0.60
